Apple AirTag
A small Bluetooth tracker that helps people find lost belongings like keys or bags.

It's coin-sized, sticks to or slips inside almost anything, and reports its location through a network of nearby Apple devices. That last part is what makes it interesting for this lesson.

Strategy: Scenario Analysis

Most product reviews ask what a product does. This one asks who's around it. You imagine real situations — people, tensions, relationships — and check whether the product changes what's possible in those situations. Misuse rarely shows up in the spec sheet. It shows up in the scenario.

Motive
A reason to misuse it.
Access
Proximity to the target.
Opportunity
What the product newly enables.

When all three line up, a product becomes a misuse vector.

You watched a situation and checked it against the same lens. When you're designing a new product — or evaluating one — don't start with the features. Start with the people around it, and ask: motive, access, opportunity. If all three line up somewhere, you've found a misuse vector before it finds you.
