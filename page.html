This tool gives you the instinct to see misuse early — before a product ships, before an attacker finds it, and before it becomes your problem to fix.

Step 07 — Story #2

The Discarded Goldmine

📦 The Setup

When a company's lease ends or the machine breaks, the printer is sold at auction or sent to a recycler — hard drive still inside, full of data.

💣 The Attack

An attacker buys the machine for $50, pulls the hard drive out, plugs it into a cheap SATA-to-USB adapter, and uses free forensic software to recover every scan, fax, and print job ever stored.

📋 The Result

Every sensitive document that ever passed through that printer is now in the attacker's hands — for the price of a pizza.

🎙️ Real World: A CBS News investigation bought used printers from a warehouse and recovered thousands of pages of medical records, social security numbers, and police warrants still on the drives.
Step 08 — What's the Fix?

How do you solve this?

✅ During Use

Use Disk Overwrite Kits — electronically shred every bit of data the moment each print job finishes.

✅ End of Life

Physically destroy the hard drive with a drill before the machine leaves the building permanently.

⚠️ Tempting but Wasteful

Replacing the printer every year to avoid data buildup — expensive, unsustainable, and the old drive still exists.

⚠️ Tempting but Wasteful

Switching to a storage-free offline printer — kills all modern workflow features and just shifts the risk elsewhere.

Step 09 — Key Takeaways

What did we learn?

01
Clearly define the main objective and side objectives of your engineering tool.
02
Clearly list out all the components required for that tool.
03
Understand each component's functioning — not just what it does, but how it stores and transmits data.
04
Research past misuse of those components in the real world before you deploy.
05
Think of possible future misuses in your system — both while active and at end of life.
