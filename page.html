Step 03 — What is a Printer?

The Office Printer

A printer's primary purpose is to convert digital data into a permanent physical document on paper. In an office it is a shared hub for printing, scanning, and copying.
⚙️
Mechanical Engine
Uses a laser and 180°C fuser to bond toner to paper at high speeds.
🌐
Internet Connectivity
Connects to the office network so any user can print or scan remotely.
💾
Hard Disk Storage
Buffers large print jobs and retains a copy of every document processed.
🧠
Embedded SoC
The "brain" — runs the OS, processes images, coordinates mechanical timing.
📷
Image Sensor (Scanner)
Converts physical pages into digital files for email or cloud storage.

Step 05 — Story #1

The Invisible Proxy Attack

Printers on office Wi-Fi or Ethernet are a silent entry point into the entire network.

🔍 The Attack

Hackers scan the network for printers with default admin passwords like admin / 1111. Once inside, they install a small malicious script — without ever touching the hardware.

💣 The Payload

The printer is reprogrammed to BCC (Carbon Copy) every outgoing scan and every incoming print job to an external server the attacker controls.

📋 The Result

The hacker receives a digital copy of every sensitive contract as it is signed — while employees see a perfectly normal printer doing its job.

Step 08 — Story #2

The Discarded Goldmine

📦 The Setup

When a company's lease ends or the machine breaks, the printer is sold at auction or sent to a recycler — hard drive still inside, full of data.

💣 The Attack

An attacker buys the machine for $50, pulls the hard drive out, plugs it into a cheap SATA-to-USB adapter, and uses free forensic software to recover every scan, fax, and print job ever stored.

📋 The Result

Every sensitive document that ever passed through that printer is now in the attacker's hands — for the price of a pizza.

🎙️ Real World: A CBS News investigation bought used printers from a warehouse and recovered thousands of pages of medical records, social security numbers, and police warrants still sitting on the drives.
Step 09 — What's the Fix?

How do you solve this?

✅ During Use

Use Disk Overwrite Kits — electronically shred every bit of data the moment each print job finishes.

✅ End of Life

Physically destroy the hard drive with a drill before the machine leaves the building permanently.

Step 10 — Key Takeaways

What did we learn?

01
Clearly define the main objective and side objectives of your engineering tool.
02
Clearly list out all the components required for that tool.
03
Understand each component's functioning — not just what it does, but how it stores and transmits data.
04
Research past misuse of those components in the real world before you deploy.
05
Think of possible future misuses in your system — both while active and at end of life.
